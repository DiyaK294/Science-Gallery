World Science Day - Scientists Gallery

Celebrating great minds who have changed the world with their contributions to science.

Albert Einstein

Theoretical physicist known for the theory of relativity.

Marie Curie

Pioneered research on radioactivity and first female Nobel laureate.

Isaac Newton

Mathematician and physicist who formulated the laws of motion and universal gravitation.

Rosalind Franklin

Contributed to the discovery of the DNA double helix structure.

Nikola Tesla

Inventor and electrical engineer known for alternating current (AC) systems.

Jane Goodall

Primatologist and anthropologist famous for her studies on chimpanzees.

Stephen Hawking

Renowned theoretical physicist known for his work on black holes and cosmology.

Ada Lovelace

First computer programmer and visionary of computing machines.

Galileo Galilei

Father of observational astronomy, known for his work on heliocentrism.

Rachel Carson

Marine biologist and conservationist who advanced the global environmental movement.

Gregor Mendel

Father of genetics, known for his pea plant experiments.

Katherine Johnson

Mathematician whose calculations were critical for NASA's space missions.

Carl Sagan

Astronomer and science communicator, famous for his work on the cosmos.

Richard Feynman

Physicist known for quantum mechanics and charismatic teaching style.

Barbara McClintock

Geneticist known for discovering transposable elements in DNA.

Alexander Fleming

Discovered penicillin, the first widely effective antibiotic.

Niels Bohr

Contributed to the understanding of atomic structure and quantum theory.

Dorothy Hodgkin

Advanced the field of X-ray crystallography to study biomolecules.

Max Planck

Father of quantum theory, revolutionizing physics.

Chien-Shiung Wu

Experimental physicist known as the 'First Lady of Physics.'

Alfred Nobel

Inventor of dynamite and founder of the Nobel Prizes.

Florence Nightingale

Pioneering statistician and founder of modern nursing.

James Clerk Maxwell

Formulated the classical theory of electromagnetic radiation.

Charles Darwin

Developed the theory of evolution by natural selection.
